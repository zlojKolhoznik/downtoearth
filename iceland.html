Everybody needs power. But some ways we generate & use power can have a dramatic effect on the enviroment. This Energy Awareness Web-Page provides a number of clues and tips how to make smart energy choises, save money and have less impact on enviroment.

Energy literacy: Energy Literacy is an understanding of the nature and role of energy in our lives and the ability to apply this understanding to answer questions and solve problems.

Energy efficiency: More than 90% of the energy we use comes from fossil fuels that are nonrenewable and cause pollution. One of the greatest energy resources we have at our fingertips is energy efficiency, or the energy that would otherwise be wasted. Choose the most energy-efficient equipment.

Renewable energy: Renewable energy does not pollute the environment and can be continually replenished. Here are some of the major renewable resources being used today:

Biomass is any organic material that can be burned or converted to ethanol or methane.
Ethanol is used as vehicle fuel, and methane is captured from decaying garbage and waste
to produce energy.

Wind energy is used to turn the blades connected to a turbine to proudce electricity. Many places have wind resources powerful and steady enough to harness.

Solar energy is the Sun's radiant energy that can be absorbed, stored, and released by subctances or converted directly into electricity using photovoltaig (PV) cells.

Geothermal energy is produced in the Earth's core. Low-temperature geothermal can be used to heat and cool building interior spaces. High-temperature geothermal resources are recovered with wells or pipes deep underground and can be used to heat buildings or produce electricity.

Hydropower is one of the oldest power sources on the planet. Flowing water, directed through a dam or other structure, is used to spin a wheel or turbine to produce electricity or for mechanical tasks like grinding grain. Scientists and engineers are also working to develop new hydrokinetic technologies to produce electricity from the motion of waves, tides, and river or ocean currents.
